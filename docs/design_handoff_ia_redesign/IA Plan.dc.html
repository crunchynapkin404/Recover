Every page, one job.

A page-by-page information-architecture plan for Recover. Diagnosis of why the app feels wrong today, a set of rules borrowed from Whoop / Garmin / Athlytic / intervals.icu, three navigation options, and the full content plan for every screen — before any pixels move.

Basis: full source read of all 11 pages Persona: data-literate cyclist, 80% mobile Next: hi-fi mockups per page
01

Diagnosis — why it feels wrong

"Too much and too little"

Home stacks 13 modules, and 7 of them are copies of other pages (week plan, races, sessions, milestones, tags, reviews). Meanwhile the numbers you actually open the app for — HRV, RHR, sleep, TSB — are two taps deep inside an accordion.

"Too much clicking"

v0.19 applied progressive disclosure inside pages: primary vitals live behind collapsed accordions. Disclosure should mean navigate to the page that owns the topic — never expand a box to see today's number.

"Fields too big for the data"

The glass mega-card (2rem radius, generous padding) is used for everything — even a card holding two numbers. One value per hero is right; one value per 300px card is why a screen holds 3 facts. Data needs a dense tile-and-row grammar.

02

Rules — deep-learned from the apps you named

R1

One job per page, stated in one sentence

If a module doesn't serve the sentence, it moves to the page whose sentence it serves. This single rule removes 7 of Home's 13 modules.

R2

Glance beats click for core vitals — Whoop

Whoop's home puts its three dials plus HRV/RHR right on the surface; deep-dives are separate pages, not expanders. No accordion may hide a today-value.

R3

Density is a dial per page — intervals.icu vs Whoop

Today reads calm: one verdict, few words. Train & Body read dense: tables, small tiles, hairline rows — you know what TSB means; the UI should stop re-explaining it in 300px cards.

R4

Coach content gets one inbox

Briefs, insights, weekly reviews, monthly reports, debriefs — today they're scattered as Home cards. They're all coach-authored: one chronological inbox on Coach; Home shows only today's 3-line brief.

R5

Rituals are flows, not pages — Garmin morning report

Morning check-in and post-ride debrief arrive as push → bottom sheet, prefilled, ≤3 taps to done. They stop being destinations you must remember to visit.

R6

A metric renders as a card once, as a chip everywhere else

Readiness may echo on Train as a small chip ("66 · amber"), never as a second hero. Kills the double-parse without losing context.

03

Three navigation options

OPTION A

Same tabs, strict jobs

HomePlanLogCoachJournalMenu

Keep all six tabs; only redistribute content by rule R1 and flatten accordions. Zero relearning cost.

  • Cheapest to ship, purely additive/subtractive
  • Health stays orphaned off mobile nav
  • Plan vs Log split still forces two visits per training question
Recommended
OPTION B

Today · Train · Coach · Body

TodayTrainCoachBodyMenu

Train = Plan + Log merged (Week · History · Fitness segments). Body = wellness trends + sleep + journal insights + Health merged. Check-in becomes a bottom sheet launched from Today.

  • Every question has exactly one destination
  • Health data finally reachable on phone
  • Journal stops being a tab you forget; becomes a 60s ritual
  • Medium build: two merged pages, one new sheet
OPTION C

4 tabs + action button

HomeTrain+CoachYou

Whoop's current pattern: dense scrollable Home, and a center + that opens every capture action — check-in, log activity, debrief, note to coach.

  • All capture actions one thumb-reach away
  • Cleanest possible tab bar
  • Home must carry trends too → gets long again unless ruthless
  • The + can layer onto Option B later — not either/or
04

Page-by-page plan (Option B)

Today

"Can I train hard today, and what's on?"

Answer visible in ≤5 seconds, above the fold, zero taps.

1Verdict: readiness dial + band + one honest sentence of why ("HRV 64 vs 65 baseline · TSB −1.9") — the morning-brief narrative merges into the hero, not a separate card.
2Vitals strip: HRV · RHR · Sleep · TSB as four compact tiles — value, 7-day delta, sparkline. Always visible, never in an accordion. Tap a tile → Body, scrolled to that trend.
3Today's session: workout, duration, intensity, plus the adaptation note if readiness moved it. Inline actions: done / move / shrink. Pending-debrief chip attaches here.
4Coach brief: today's 3 lines, tap → Coach thread. Race chip appears here only inside 21 days out. "Check in" button opens the 60-second sheet.
Leaves this page → week strip → Train weekly review → Coach recent sessions → Train sleep stages · battery → Body milestones · tags → Body

"Plan the week, review the work."

Plan + Log merged. Readiness echoes only as a chip (R6).

Structure already works (v0.19/0.20 accordion-per-domain + hairline rows is right — settings is archival, so accordions belong here). 05

The three rituals — designed as flows, not pages

Morning · ~90 sec

1Push: "Readiness 66 · amber — HRV dipped"
2Open → Today answers in 5s: verdict + vitals + session
3"Check in" sheet: 3 prefilled sliders, pre-toggled tags, optional note — 60s, done
4Skim the coach brief; reply if needed

Post-ride · your explicit ask

1Sync lands the ride → push: "How was Zwift Race?"
2Tap → debrief bottom sheet over Today: RPE 1–10 chips, feel chips, note / voice dictation
3Coach writes the debrief review, quoting your words next to the numbers
4Review lands in the Coach inbox; chip clears from Today

Week start · Sunday evening

1Push: "Plan next week — 3 min"
2Train / Week: availability intake, calendar-prefilled
3Week materializes; taper/race constraints applied
4Monday's Today shows the first session
06

Density grammar — fixing "fields too big"

One heroMax one glass mega-card per screen — the page's focal metric. Everything else: flat tiles and hairline rows.
Stat tile≤64px tall: 10px caps label, 20–22px value, delta + sparkline on the right. Four fit in one phone row-pair.
Rows, not cardsLists (activities, biomarkers, adjustments) are 48–56px hairline rows. A ride is a row; a ride detail is a page.
Radius scaleHero 24px · tile 12px · row 0. The current uniform 2rem is what makes small data feel inflated.
Accordion policyOnly for archival content: adjustment history, remaining skeleton, settings domains. Never for a today-value.
