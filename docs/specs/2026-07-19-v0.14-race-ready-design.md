# v0.14 — Race Ready: Design

**Date:** 2026-07-19
**Status:** Approved

## Goal

The adaptive week manages training; race day is why it exists. Races become
first-class entities, the final skeleton weeks reshape into a taper, and the
athlete gets a defensible race-day form projection that updates daily. Done
when: an athlete with a race in 8 weeks watches the plan taper into it and
gets a projection with honest uncertainty — never a fabricated readiness
number.

## Principle (unchanged from the brand)

Forecasting from fabricated CTL would be fabrication with extra steps —
everything here stands on v0.10's honest load engine. The projection is a
**form outlook** (TSB via the readiness engine's form mapping), never a
projected readiness score: HRV and RHR cannot be forecast, and pretending
otherwise is the exact dishonesty this app exists to avoid. Uncertainty is a
two-scenario band computed from the athlete's own adherence history, not a
decorative confidence interval. Stored CTL/ATL null → `insufficient`, shown
as a calibrating-style state, never a number.

## Schema (migration 0016, additive)

- **`races`** table: `id`, `userId` (cascade), `name`, `raceType` (free
  text, the same vocabulary `generateTrainingPlan` already parses —
  "marathon", "70.3", "gran fondo"…), `sport` (nullable; inferred via
  `inferSports` when absent), `date`, `priority` (`A` | `B` | `C`),
  `status` (`upcoming` | `completed` | `skipped`, default `upcoming`),
  `goalNote` (nullable free text — feeds the race brief),
  `resultActivityId` (nullable FK → activities, set by the debrief),
  `debriefedAt` (nullable timestamp — the debrief's once-only guard, set
  on both the activity-found and the 48 h no-activity paths),
  `createdAt`/`updatedAt`. Unique on `(userId, date, name)`; index on
  `(userId, status, date)`.
- **`training_plans.race_id`** (nullable FK → races, `on delete set
null`): the A race the plan targets. Existing `raceType`/`raceDate`
  columns stay as the generation snapshot; a deleted race never breaks a
  plan. No backfill of old plans.

## Race lifecycle

- `generateTrainingPlan` accepts an optional `raceId`; given one it reads
  type/date from the race. Called the old way (inline raceType +
  raceDate), it **creates an A race implicitly** and links it — every new
  plan's target race lands on the calendar automatically.
- Races are created/edited via the /plan Races section and coach tools.
- `completed` is set by the post-race debrief (which links the matching
  activity); `skipped` is a manual out. Creating a race in the past is a
  validation error.
- The informal coach-memory `race` type stays for chatter; the persona
  prompt gets one line telling the coach the races table is the source of
  truth.

## Pure engines

### Taper — `materializeWeek` gains an optional `race` input

`{ date, priority, raceType }`, threaded from the service layer (the
engine derives race proximity from `date` vs `weekStart` — no redundant
day-count input). Rules:

- **A race, inside the taper window** — window by distance: 21 days
  (marathon / Ironman), 14 (half / 70.3 / fondo / century), 10 (short
  course: 5k / 10k / crit / sprint+olympic tri). The week's phase is
  forced to `taper` and the target load is cut from the athlete's
  **current actual load** (previous week's `actualLoad`, falling back to
  current CTL × 7), not the skeleton: race week ≈ 45 %, week−1 ≈ 65 %,
  week−2 ≈ 80 %. Volume drops, intensity stays: taper weeks keep one
  short quality touch, and race week gets an "openers" variant. Race day
  itself becomes a `race` slot (no generated workout, availability
  ignored); the day before is forced rest or a ≤ 30 min opener.
- **Ramp guard learns to taper:** `effectiveWeekLoad`'s
  `RAMP_CLAMP_PCT` (±20 %) currently blocks any big cut. In a taper week
  the **downward** clamp is bypassed (upward still applies), recorded as
  an adjustment (`"taper: ramp guard downward clamp bypassed"`).
- **B race:** no load reshaping; the 2 days before are protected —
  quality sessions there step down via the existing `STEP_DOWN`
  machinery, the day before prefers rest — and race day becomes a `race`
  slot.
- **C race:** race day becomes a `race` slot replacing whatever was
  there; nothing else moves.
- **Two races in one week:** highest priority wins the reshaping; the
  other still gets its day slot.
- A race beyond the plan horizon does not taper anything (countdown
  only).

### Forecast — `src/lib/race/forecast.ts` (new, pure)

- `forecastForm(inputs)` forward-simulates the same EMA the honest load
  engine uses (reusing `CTL_DAYS`/`ATL_DAYS` from `training-load.ts`):
  seeds with today's stored CTL/ATL, steps daily over planned loads from
  the materialized week plus the remaining (taper-reshaped) skeleton
  weeks, out to race day. Output: per-day projected CTL/ATL/TSB and a
  race-day summary.
- **Two scenarios, one honest band:** _full execution_ (planned loads
  as-is) and _recent adherence_ (each load × trailing 4-week adherence
  fraction, floored at 0.5). The spread between the two race-day TSBs is
  the stated range; the band label comes from the readiness engine's
  form mapping applied to each end.
- Stored CTL/ATL null → returns `{ insufficient: true }`; every consumer
  renders the calibrating-style empty state. All copy says "projection".
- No active plan → no load series to walk; consumers show "no plan
  targets this race" with a CTA, not a decay fantasy. A race past the
  plan's end projects only to plan end and says so.

### What-if — `simulatePlanChange` (same module, pure)

- `simulatePlanChange(week, change, forecastInputs)` where `change` is a
  move / swap / skip of a day; returns `{ before, after }` forecasts and
  the week-load delta. With no race on file the delta anchors on
  end-of-week TSB, so the feature works for every athlete.

## Services & scheduler

- `src/lib/race/service.ts`: race CRUD, next-upcoming lookup, and the
  assembly of `forecastForm` inputs (current metrics + open week +
  skeleton remainder).
- Week rollover / materialization threads the nearest relevant race into
  `materializeWeek`.
- **Race-day brief:** the scheduler's morning-insight step checks for a
  race today (any priority). If found, the prompt gets a race context
  block — goal note, taper summary (planned vs executed taper load),
  today's actual readiness, projected vs actual TSB — and instructions to
  write a race brief, not generic advice. Same thread, same slot,
  deterministic data assembly.
- **Post-race debrief:** on scheduler ticks after race day, once an
  activity matching the race is ingested (same date, activity sport in
  the race's `inferSports` set) — or after 48 h without one — the debrief
  runs exactly once, guarded by `debriefedAt` (same class of
  due-since-slot logic as the weekly review). Activity found: link it,
  set `status = completed`, post a coach-thread message comparing
  forecast vs actual morning readiness, taper plan vs execution, and race
  stats vs the athlete's typical. 48 h with no activity: post an honest
  "no data landed, mark it yourself" message; the race stays `upcoming`
  (status is the user's call — finished, or skipped) but `debriefedAt`
  still sets, so the message never repeats.
- Strava firewall unchanged: race-linked Strava activities are excluded
  from every AI surface exactly as everywhere else (`ne(provider,
'strava')`); the debrief's "race stats vs typical" uses non-Strava data
  only, and says so when that leaves nothing.

## Coach tools (49 → 53)

- `get_races` (read) — list with status/priority filters.
- `upsert_race`, `delete_race` (`write:plan`).
- `simulate_plan_change` (read-only — it never saves).
- `get_week_plan` output gains `race` slots.

## UI

- **Dashboard:** `RaceCountdownCard` — next upcoming race: name, priority,
  days out, and the projected race-day TSB **range** with its band (one
  band when both scenarios agree, "amber–green" when they straddle; or
  the calibrating/insufficient state, or the "no plan targets this race"
  CTA).
- **/plan:** a Races section (list + add/edit dialog); race and taper
  markers on the week strip; per-day **move/swap controls**
  (select-based menus, no drag) whose confirm dialog shows the
  `simulatePlanChange` delta — "Race-day form: −4.1 → −1.8 TSB (still
  amber)" — before calling the existing `moveWorkout`/swap services.
- No new page.

## Testing

House pattern — engines pure and fully unit-tested:

- Taper reshaping per priority × distance × window edge; ramp-guard
  downward bypass (and upward clamp retained); race-slot placement;
  day-before rest; B-race step-down; C-race replacement; two races in
  one week.
- Forecast EMA forward-sim against hand-computed values; adherence floor;
  scenario band ordering; `insufficient` propagation; plan-horizon cap.
- `simulatePlanChange` deltas for move/swap/skip, with and without a
  race.
- DB-gated service/scheduler tests: implicit A-race creation on plan
  generation, debrief idempotency and 48 h no-activity path, brief
  triggering on race morning.
- Tool registry count test 49 → 53; tool contract tests per house
  pattern.

## Error handling

- Race in the past → validation error on create.
- Race beyond plan horizon → countdown only; forecast capped at plan end
  and labelled.
- No active plan → countdown card with CTA; no forecast.
- Deleted race → `training_plans.race_id` nulls; plan runs on snapshot
  columns.
- CTL/ATL missing → `insufficient` everywhere a projection would show.

## Out of scope

- Pace/power race-execution targets (pacing plans) — coach chatter only.
- Multi-race season periodization (reverse periodization from a race
  series).
- Forecasting readiness (HRV/RHR) — permanently out, by principle.
- Drag-and-drop plan editing.
